Business Documents Guide

ProjectZero generates business documents in two phases: Discovery (before building the product) and Planning (after building). The /business-docs command drives both modes.

Planning (After Build)
Build Phase

Before Build — Discovery

TAM-SAM-SOM Analysis

Market sizing analysis that quantifies the Total Addressable Market, Serviceable Addressable Market, and Serviceable Obtainable Market for the product.
Needs
Product vision, target industry, geographic scope, pricing assumptions
Produces
Market size figures, growth projections, segment breakdown, addressable market rationale

Competitive Analysis

Comprehensive evaluation of the competitive landscape including direct competitors, indirect alternatives, and market positioning opportunities.
Needs
Product category, target market, known competitors, feature priorities
Produces
Competitor matrix, feature comparison, SWOT analysis, differentiation strategy

Team Composition

Defines the optimal team structure needed to build and launch the product, with roles, seniority levels, and hiring timeline.
Needs
Product scope, tech stack, timeline, budget constraints
Produces
Org chart, role descriptions, hiring plan, team cost estimates

Business Model

Defines how the product creates, delivers, and captures value. Covers revenue streams, pricing strategy, and unit economics.
Needs
Product vision, target users, market analysis, competitive positioning
Produces
Business model canvas, revenue model, pricing tiers, unit economics, CAC/LTV analysis

After Build — Planning

Financial Projections

3–5 year financial model including revenue forecasts, expense projections, cash flow analysis, and key financial metrics.
Needs
Business model, pricing, market size, team costs, infrastructure costs
Produces
P&L projection, cash flow statement, break-even analysis, key metrics dashboard

Costing

Detailed cost breakdown for building, launching, and operating the product across all categories—development, infrastructure, team, and operations.
Needs
Architecture decisions, team composition, tech stack, infrastructure plan
Produces
Cost breakdown by category, monthly burn rate, runway calculation, cost optimization recommendations

GTM Strategy

Go-to-market plan covering launch strategy, customer acquisition channels, partnerships, and growth milestones.
Needs
Product features, target personas, competitive analysis, business model, budget
Produces
Launch plan, channel strategy, marketing calendar, partnership targets, growth metrics

Pitch Deck

Investor-ready presentation covering problem, solution, market, traction, business model, team, financials, and the ask.
Needs
All discovery docs, financial projections, product demo/screenshots, team bios
Produces
12–15 slide deck structure, narrative arc, key data points, appendix materials

Investor Data Room

Organized collection of all documents an investor needs during due diligence—financials, legal, product, and team documentation.
Needs
All business docs, legal documents, product metrics, team information
Produces
Structured data room index, document checklist, access-controlled folder structure, summary memo

How It Works

1. Choose Mode
Run /business-docs and select Discovery or Planning mode based on your current phase.
2. Select Documents
Pick which documents to generate. You can generate all at once or one at a time as inputs become available.
3. Provide Context
The workflow prompts for required inputs. Previously generated documents are automatically used as context for new ones.
4. Review & Finalize
Each document goes through a review stage. Approved documents are saved to the product's docs/business/ directory.
